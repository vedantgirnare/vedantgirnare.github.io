Hi, I am
Vedant Girnare!

I like Mathematics, especially probability and measure theory. Beyond my quantitative pursuits, I am deeply engaged in literature and observational astronomy.

I am Vedant Girnare, a Mathematics major pursuing a BS-MS degree at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Bhopal (IISERB). My academic and research endeavors are heavily rooted in advanced probability, stochastic calculus, and measure theory, with a strong focus on quantitative finance and operations research. I am currently working on my MS thesis involving optimal stopping and viscosity solutions, utilizing tools like Python and MATLAB to translate theoretical market mechanics into numerical realities. Alongside my mathematical pursuits, I had the privilege of serving as the Coordinator of IBAC, the Astronomy Club of IISERB, for the 2024-25 tenure, fostering scientific engagement within the student community.

Beyond research and coding, I maintain a highly disciplined routine centered around deep focus, running, and yoga. I am an avid reader of classical literature and philosophy, frequently immersing myself in the complex narratives of authors like Dostoevsky, Kafka, and Salman Rushdie. Additionally, I am actively studying Japanese with a dedicated goal of achieving advanced proficiency. Whether I am writing essays that bridge the gap between philosophy and mathematics or tackling advanced differential equations, I approach my work with a commitment to continuous learning and intellectual rigor.
